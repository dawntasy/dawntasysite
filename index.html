About

The Short Version

Hi, I'm Jasper.

In January 2025, I published my first novel. I was thirteen.

A month later, I composed its soundtrack and released it worldwide. By July, I'd fallen down the rabbit hole of artificial intelligence and didn't come back up. By September, I was competing against university students in AI hackathons—and winning. More recently, I was offered a research internship at a medical AI startup working on Parkinson's disease.

Somewhere in the middle of all that, I started Year 9.

I don't think of these as separate projects. The novel asked a question. The music gave it feeling. The AI is trying to find the answer.

Everything I build lives under one roof: Dawntasy. Not because it's a brand, but because it all comes from the same belief—that one person, moving fast enough, can build something the world hasn't seen yet.

Right now, I'm writing a deep learning research paper targeting NeurIPS, improving machine learning models for Parkinson's metabolomics, and still figuring out what happens in Book Two.

I'm just getting started.

The clocks have no hands. The tyrant has no face. And the bear who swore revenge may be the true villain of this story.

A bear watches his parents die. He makes a promise. He spends twenty years sharpening it into a weapon.

His name is Yaee. The tyrant who took everything from him is called Time Smith—a dictator so powerful he can silence cities, so feared that no one alive has ever seen his face. Yaee joins a rebellion. He finds allies. He loses his mentor. He fights his way to the heart of the regime.

And then he burns an entire city to the ground with its people still inside.

This is not the story you think it is.

Because when Yaee finally stands before Time Smith, weapon raised, ready to collect on twenty years of rage—the tyrant doesn't fight back. He sits down. And he tells Yaee the truth.

A truth so dangerous that only three people in the world are allowed to know it. A truth that turns every clock backward, every ally into a question mark, and every certainty to ash.

The clocks in this world have no hands.
Now you know why.

Frequently Asked Questions

Jasper Jiang is a 13-year-old published author, musical composer, and AI researcher from Sydney, Australia. He published his debut YA fantasy novel Dawntasy: The Circular Dawn — Time's True Name in January 2025 and donates 100% of proceeds to the Make-A-Wish Foundation. He also works as an AI research intern at Preview Health, researching Parkinson's disease using deep learning.

Dawntasy: The Circular Dawn — Time's True Name is a YA fantasy novel about a bear named Yaee who seeks revenge against a dictator called Time Smith. It explores themes of revenge, truth, and the cost of knowing too much. The book has earned 4.8 stars on Amazon and is praised for its phenomenal writing, vivid world-building, and unpredictable plot twists.

You can purchase the eBook on Amazon. You can also find it on Goodreads and The StoryGraph. 100% of proceeds are donated to the Make-A-Wish Foundation.

Jasper works as an AI research intern at Preview Health, using deep learning to improve machine learning models for Parkinson's disease metabolomics (specifically CRANK-MS for metabolite identification). He is also authoring a deep learning research paper called FunCAN (Functional Complexity Adaptive Networks) targeting submission to NeurIPS, one of the world's most prestigious AI conferences.

Dawntasy is the umbrella for all of Jasper Jiang's creative and research work — spanning fiction, music composition, and artificial intelligence research. It represents the belief that one person, moving fast enough, can build something the world hasn't seen yet.

Yes. Jasper won 3rd Place at the UNSW 2025 Societal Impact of AI Symposium and the Most Innovative Award at the ACU Air Aware Hackathon — competing against university students as a Year 9 high school student.
